## Design Context

### Users
Portuguese-speaking users with food allergies checking what they can safely eat at restaurants. High-stakes, health-critical context — a mistake is not just inconvenient, it could be medically dangerous. Used in the moment, standing in a restaurant, often under time pressure.

### Brand Personality
Trust · Clarity · Restraint

The app must feel trustworthy first, beautiful second. Every visual choice should reduce cognitive load, not add decoration. The H3 restaurant screen goes further — it mirrors H3's own brand identity: "New Hamburgology," photography-first, restrained H3 blue (#1a9ed4), heavy uppercase typesetting.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light — used in bright restaurant environments on mobile screens
- **Visual tone**: Editorial/magazine for H3 screens; clean clinical trust-feel for generic screens
- **Typography**: System font stack (no custom fonts installed) — compensate with weight, tracking, and uppercase
- **References**: H3 website (h3.com/ementa/) — photography-first, centered dividers, dark footer
- **Anti-references**: Nothing that looks like a generic health/diet app; no pastel greens everywhere; no admin dashboard side-stripe cards

### Design Principles
1. **Photography first** — food images are the hero; UI chrome should step back
2. **Signal over decoration** — every visual element communicates allergen risk, not just looks nice
3. **Restraint as brand** — H3 blue (#1a9ed4) appears sparingly; overuse kills its power
4. **Typography over color** — hierarchy through weight and tracking, not color variety
5. **Dark footer as signature** — the #171717 footer with "New Hamburgology" is a brand anchor; maintain it
