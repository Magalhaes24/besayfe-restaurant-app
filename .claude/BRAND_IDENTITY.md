# besayfe — Brand Identity Guide

## Executive Summary

**besayfe** is a health-first technology platform designed to empower individuals with food allergies by providing instant risk assessment of products through barcode scanning and label photography. The brand embodies safety, trust, simplicity, and proactive health management with a human-centered approach.

---

## 1. Brand Essence

### Name
**besayfe** (play on "be safe") — a direct, approachable name that communicates the core promise: being safe around food.

### Mission
To make food allergen safety accessible, transparent, and actionable for individuals and groups managing dietary restrictions.

### Core Values
- **Trust** — Reliable data and honest risk assessment
- **Empowerment** — Giving users control over their health decisions
- **Community** — Shared allergen visibility with friends and groups
- **Simplicity** — Friction-free scanning and instant results
- **Proactivity** — Prevention through early detection

### 2.2 Color Psychology in Context

**Greens in besayfe:**
- Represent health, wellness, and "safe to consume"

- Create a sense of calm and trust in health decisions
- Contrast with warnings (red) for clear risk hierarchy

**Neutrals in besayfe:**
- Ensure readability and accessibility
- Create visual breathing room around critical information
- Support data visualization (risk scores, allergen charts)

## 3. Logo & Mark System

### Logo Variations

#### Primary Logo Assets
- **besayfe-logo.png** — Full color logo (green + dark text)
- **besayfe-logo-white.png** — Monochrome white, for dark backgrounds
- **Y-besayfe-white.png** — Symbol-only variant (stylized "Y"), for app icon
- **Y-besayfe-green.png** — Symbol-only variant in accent green

### Logo Design Philosophy

The logo incorporates a stylized "Y" shape suggesting:
- A **immunoglobulin** (antibodie responsible for allergies)

The form is:
- **Clean and geometric** for digital clarity
- **Scalable** from icon (16px) to billboard sizes
- **Memorable** with a unique, non-generic silhouette
- **Adaptable** to different color modes and backgrounds

### Icon Usage
- App icon: Monochrome "Y" symbol
- Splash screen: Full "besayfe" logo on accent green background (#73AC84)
- Status bar: Can accommodate both variations depending on layout

## 4. Typography

### Font Family Strategy

**System Fonts** (no custom typeface dependencies)
- **Web:** `system-ui, -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, 'Segoe UI', Roboto, Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif`
- **iOS:** System fonts with `UIFontDescriptorSystemDesignDefault` (San Francisco family)
- **Android:** Native system font stack
- **Web Rounded:** Optional rounded variant for friendly headings (`SF Pro Rounded`, `Hiragino Maru Gothic ProN`)

### Rationale
- **Native feel** on each platform (iOS users expect San Francisco, Android users expect Roboto)
- **Performance** — No font file downloads, instant rendering
- **Accessibility** — System fonts are optimized for readability
- **Trust** — Familiar typography doesn't distract from critical health information

### Typography Hierarchy (Conceptual)
1. **Headings** — Bold, large, accent green for primary sections
2. **Body text** — Gray 700, system sans-serif, 14-16px
3. **Labels** — Gray 500, smaller, secondary information
4. **Alerts/Risks** — Bold, gray 800, with error red for danger states

## 5. Forms & Design Elements

### Buttons

#### Primary Button
- **Color:** Accent Green (#73AC84)
- **Text:** White
- **Shape:** Rounded corners (8-12px radius)
- **Usage:** Main calls-to-action (Scan, Submit, Confirm)

#### Secondary Button
- **Color:** Accent Green Light Light (#E3EEE6) background
- **Text:** Accent Green (#73AC84)
- **Shape:** Rounded corners
- **Usage:** Alternative actions, cancel options

#### Error Button
- **Color:** Error Red (#B42318)
- **Text:** White
- **Shape:** Rounded corners
- **Usage:** Delete, decline, confirm dangerous actions

### Cards & Containers
- **Background:** White (#FFFFFF) or Accent Green Light Light (#E3EEE6)
- **Border:** Gray 300 (#E4E7EC) subtle dividers
- **Padding:** Consistent 16px internal spacing
- **Radius:** 8-12px for soft, approachable look
- **Shadow:** Minimal elevation for layering without harshness

### Risk Score Visualization
- **Color Gradient:** HSL interpolation from 120° (green) → 0° (red)
  - Safe (0-33): Green hues
  - Warning (34-66): Yellow/orange hues
  - Danger (67-100): Red hues
- **Display:** Circular gauge, percentage, or bar chart depending on context
- **Text:** Bold, gray 800, with supporting risk message

### Dividers & Separators
- **Color:** Gray 300 (#E4E7EC)
- **Weight:** 1px
- **Usage:** Visual separation between content blocks without creating harsh divisions

## 7. Tone of Voice & Emotional Resonance

### Brand Voice Characteristics
| Dimension | Expression |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Trustworthy** | Data-driven, transparent, honest about risk |
| **Empowering** | You're in control, informed decisions |
| **Approachable** | Friendly, no jargon, human language |
| **Proactive** | Prevention-focused, not reactive |
| **Protective** | Alert you to danger, never minimize risk |

### Emotional Goals
- **Security:** Users feel safe with their allergen data
- **Confidence:** Informed decisions reduce anxiety
- **Belonging:** Friend groups create community around health
- **Relief:** Instant scanning removes product doubt
- **Pride:** Managing allergies becomes empowering, not limiting

### Message Examples

❌ **Avoid:**
- "System failure detected" (cold, technical)
- "You may have a severe allergy" (frightening)
- "Product risk: CRITICAL" (alarmist without context)

✅ **Do:**
- "High allergen match — we recommend checking the label" (actionable, informative)
- "Safe for your allergens" (affirming, positive)
- "You and 3 friends all react to shellfish" (community building)

## 9. Accessibility Considerations

### Color Contrast
- All text meets WCAG AA standard (4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text)
- Never rely solely on color to convey meaning (always pair with icons, text, patterns)

### Color-Blind Safe Palettes
- Green/red risk states are paired with icons and percentages
- No red-only or green-only indication of critical status
- Patterns or text redundancy ensures meaning beyond color

### Typography
- Minimum 16px font size for body text on mobile
- Line height 1.5x or greater for readability
- Avoid all-caps for extended text

## 10. Brand Do's and Don'ts

### ✅ Do's
- Use the primary green for interactive elements and CTAs
- Pair colors with icons or text for meaning clarity
- Keep interfaces clean with generous whitespace
- Use system fonts for optimal readability on all platforms
- Emphasize trust and transparency in messaging
- Celebrate user control and informed decisions
- Include diverse representation in imagery

### ❌ Don'ts
- Don't use accent green as primary background (too much saturation)
- Don't reduce brand colors arbitrarily in dark mode without testing
- Don't use red without clear risk context
- Don't employ jargon or medical terminology users don't understand
- Don't minimize or downplay allergen risks
- Don't create FOMO around group health features
- Don't use bright gradients or trendy effects (reduces trustworthiness)
- Don't put text on complex image backgrounds (readability)
